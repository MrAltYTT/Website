The Ordinary World

Junior’s ordinary world is the Spokane Indian Reservation, a place full of poverty and struggles. He is a 14-year-old boy with physical challenges, is bullied and often feels lonely. His family loves him, but they face financial problems like many others on the reservation. Junior’s world is full of hopes: he loves drawing cartoons and dreams of a better future, but he lives in a community where dreams are often crushed by problems like alcoholism. This everyday life prepares Junior for his journey, where he will grow and change.

Call to Adventure

During a class, Junior discovers that his geometry textbook is the same one his mother used 30 years earlier. This realization symbolizes the lack of resources and opportunities on the reservation, making Junior feel trapped in a cycle of poverty. In frustration, he throws the book and accidentally hits his teacher, Mr. P. Later, he visits Junior and urges him to leave the reservation, telling him he deserves a better life. This becomes his call to adventure as it serves as a turning point for his life, giving him the push in order to start his adventure to Reardan High School. Mr. P is the Guardian of Juniors journey, as he is the one who instructs Junior to leave the reservation.

The Tempter

Rowdy serves as the tempter in Junior’s hero’s journey. As Junior’s best friend, he symbolizes the resistance to change and loyalty to his home. When Junior decides to leave the reservation for a better education and future, Rowdy feels betrayed and reacts with anger, resulting in him attempting to fight Junior. His actions force Junior to make a choice between The Reservation and his journey.

The Threshold

The Threshold is the 22 miles between Junior's home on the Spokane Reservation and the wealthy school in Reardan, which is made up of white people. This distance represents the cultural gap he must cross to pursue a better future. On his first day, Junior feels like an outsider as everyone stares at him, highlighting his isolation.

The Unfamiliar World

The Unfamiliar world is Reardan, the all-white school Junior transfers to. Unlike the reservation, Reardan is a place of privilege, and opportunity, but it is also filled with higher expectations. During his time there, he feels like he does not fit in, and later on, he will have to face challenges.

Challenges

Early on, Roger bullies him with a racist comment, but Junior punches him, and to his surprise, it earns Roger’s respect. As Junior starts making friends, he struggles to hide his poverty, pretending to forget lunch money or walking home so no one realizes he can’t afford a ride. Things get even harder during the first basketball game against Wellpinit, where the crowd turns on him, and Rowdy, his former best friend, injures him on the court. Then, tragedy strikes when Junior’s grandmother, one of the most important people in his life, dies after being hit by a drunk driver. Her death leaves Junior heartbroken, but he holds onto her wisdom and strength as he continues to find his way through his two worlds.

The Helpers

Roger becomes a helper to Junior by supporting him and showing kindness as Junior adjusts to life at Reardan. After their initial conflict, Roger respects Junior and even looks out for him, treating him as a friend. He offers Junior a ride home from school events when he notices Junior walking long distances, helping him hide his poverty from others.

Mentor

Junior’s grandmother is a mentor to him by using her wisdom and kindness. She’s known for being incredibly tolerant, and she teaches Junior to stay true to himself and treat others with respect. When Junior struggles with life at Reardan and the tension with his community, her lessons give him the strength to keep going. An example of this is when after the incident with Roger, her advice helps Junior understand how to handle the situation. Later on, they can become friends and Junior understands his relationship with Roger better.

The Abyss

The abyss in Junior's journey happens during the second basketball game against Wellpinit, which is a critical turning point in the story of Junior. This time, Junior and his team beat Wellpinit, and Junior plays an essential role, scoring key points and guarding Rowdy. While the victory brings Junior a sense of accomplishment, it’s also bittersweet. He realizes that despite conquering his friends on the reservation, they are all facing the same poverty and challenges that he left behind on his journey.

Transformation & Revelation

Junior transforms when he realizes he has many identities and belongs to multiple tribes/communities. He recognizes that he isn’t just a member of the Spokane tribe but also belongs to the tribes of cartoonists, basketball players, and many more. This moment of realization helps him accept his complex identity, allowing him to move forward and accept that he can no longer identify with just one group of people.

Atonement

The atonement happens when Junior begins to repair his friendship with Rowdy. After being angry for a long time, Rowdy visits Junior and opens up during a game of basketball. They acknowledge their differences, with Rowdy joking that Junior is a nomad who belongs to the world, while he belongs to the reservation. These words touch Junior, and he starts crying. This shows Junior’s acceptance of both before and after his journey.
